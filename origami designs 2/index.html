Camel

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Almost half of all known species live in Madagascar.
  • Skin crystals enable them to change color at will.

Pigeons

  1. Piegons are highly sociable animals.
  2. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  3. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet.

Teddy Bear

  • American multi-billionaire investor Paul Greenwood owned the largest collection of teddy bears.
  • The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London.
  • Animation movie giant Walt Disney produced the first colour cartoon film featuring teddy bears – Alice and the Three Bears – in 1924.

Panda

  1. Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago.
  2. Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.
  3. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • Most have red-orange eyes.
  • Cicadas don’t sing at night.
